# Trademark Policy

Our trademarks, including the Cirron name, logo, and project names, represent the quality and reliability our community expects. This document, the "Policy," defines the terms for use of Cirron, Inc.'s trademarks.

> **Note**
> While our software is available under [Apache 2.0](LICENSE.md), that license does not grant any rights to use our trademarks. This Policy is not intended to limit lawful use of our trademarks but to clarify what we consider lawful use to be.
>
> Trademark law can be ambiguous, so we aim to provide enough clarity for you to understand whether we will consider your use acceptable.

## Covered Marks

This policy covers all trademarks and service marks associated with Cirron, Inc., collectively the "Marks", whether registered or not. This includes:

- **Word Marks**: "Cirron", "Cirron SDK", "cirron-sdk", and the names of any sibling projects.
- **Logos**: the official Cirron logo and any project logos.
- **Trade Dress**: the distinctive visual appearance of our website and product UI.

Trademark rights exist even when the Mark is not registered.

## Trademark usage guidelines

Whenever you use one of the Marks, you must do so in a way that does not mislead anyone, directly or by omission, about exactly what they are getting and from whom. This is the core principle of trademark law and the rule that should guide you when this Policy doesn't address your specific case.

To avoid confusion:

- Modified software must be clearly labeled as such and not presented as the original.
- Logos and identifiers must not be used in ways that suggest endorsement or official affiliation when none exists.

## Restrictions on similar trademarks

You may not use names or marks that are too similar to ours:

- You may not use an obvious variation of any Mark, or any phonetic equivalent, foreign-language equivalent, takeoff, or abbreviation, for a similar or compatible product or service.
- You may not use or register the Marks, in whole or in part, as part of your own trademark, service mark, domain name, company name, trade name, product name, or service name.
- You may not use the Marks in connection with software that is not in compliance with the Apache 2.0 license.
- You agree that you will not acquire any rights in the Marks and that any goodwill generated by your use of the Marks accrues solely to Cirron, Inc.

## Uses we consider non-infringing

### Distribution of unmodified source code or artifacts packaged by us

Redistribution of unmodified `cirron-sdk` releases (source or built artifacts from PyPI) is allowed. You may retain the Word Marks and Logos we placed on the software when distributing an official, unchanged version.

### Distribution of modified source code or artifacts packaged by you

If you compile and distribute bitwise-identical artifacts from our unmodified source, you may retain the Marks.

For modifications (e.g. bug fixes that preserve compatibility), label your artifacts to indicate they were built by you.

Substantive changes should be treated as a fork, requiring a different package name (not `cirron-sdk` or any variant). Avoid using the Logos. You may truthfully state that "this software is derived from cirron-sdk version X.Y."

### Compatibility, interoperability, and derivation statements

You may use the Word Marks (but not the Logos) to truthfully describe the relationship between your software and ours. Place the Mark **after** a verb or preposition that describes the relationship.

Acceptable:

- *&lt;your product&gt;* **is compatible with** Cirron
- *&lt;your product&gt;* **works with** the Cirron platform
- *&lt;your product&gt;* **integrates with** cirron-sdk
- *&lt;your product&gt;* **reads** the Cirron spool format
- *&lt;your product&gt;* **exports to** Cirron

Not acceptable (these create confusion about the source):

- Cirron *&lt;your product&gt;*
- *&lt;your product&gt;* **by Cirron** (when not actually by Cirron)
- *&lt;your company&gt;* **Cirron Edition**
- Cirron - *&lt;your product&gt;*

### "Powered by Cirron"

You may say a product or service is **"powered by Cirron"** only if you are an active customer of the Cirron platform and the product genuinely depends on it (not just on the open-source SDK). Saying "powered by Cirron" because you `pip install cirron-sdk`'d once is misleading and not permitted.

If you are using only the SDK without a platform subscription, the right phrasing is "**built with cirron-sdk**", "**built with the Cirron SDK**", or "**uses cirron-sdk for profiling**."

### Community affiliation

You may use the Word Marks and Logos in non-commercial, decorative contexts (avatars, themes, conference talks given as a community member) to show your support, provided the use does not imply official representation, endorsement, or affiliation with Cirron, Inc.

### Websites

You may use the Word Marks and Logos (but not our Trade Dress) on your website to show support for Cirron, as long as:

- Your site has branding clearly distinguishable from ours.
- Your own branding is more prominent than any of our Marks.
- Logos hyperlink to [cirron.com](https://cirron.com) or this repository.
- The site does not mislead visitors into thinking it is Cirron's website, service, or product.
- The site clearly states that you are not affiliated with or endorsed by Cirron.

### Publications and presentations

You may use the Word Marks in book and article titles, and the Logo in illustrations within the document, as long as the use does not suggest that Cirron has published, endorsed, or agreed with your work.

## Uses we consider infringing

The following are likely infringing without express written permission:

- Using the Marks as part of a domain name or subdomain (`cirron-cloud.com`, `cirron-enterprise.io`, etc.).
- Using the Marks as the name for software that has been substantively modified.
- Marketing your product as an extension, plugin, or component of the Cirron platform when it is not part of an official partnership.
- Using the Logo or Trade Dress in your own product UI, paid advertisements, swag for sale, or marketing materials.
- "Cirron-certified," "the official Cirron extension for X," "an extension of the Cirron platform," or similar phrasing without written permission.
- Disparaging use, or use that suggests endorsement of unrelated political, religious, or commercial positions.

This applies equally to everyone, including competitors. A competing platform may state that their product is *compatible with* Cirron or *integrates with* the Cirron platform. They may not market the SDK as part of their own platform offering, distribute a rebranded version, or use the Cirron name or logo in their product UI or marketing.
